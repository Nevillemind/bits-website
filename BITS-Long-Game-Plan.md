## The Federated Intelligence Network — Deep Dive

### How It Works

Each BITS box processes data locally. Invoices, quotes, SOPs, scheduling — all private. Then, with client opt-in, the box generates *anonymized summaries* — not raw data, just signals:

- "Contractor in Richmond quoted concrete jobs 14% higher this month"
- "HVAC businesses in NoVA saw a 30% spike in service calls this week"
- "3 of 10 plumbing clients flagged the same supplier for delays"

These summaries are encrypted, stripped of identity, and aggregated upstream. **No raw data leaves the box. Ever.**

This is federated learning — the same architecture Google uses with phone keyboards. BITS does it for SMBs in the trades.

### What Clients Get

- **Benchmarking:** "Your close rate is 38%. Regional average is 52%. Here's the gap."
- **Market timing:** "Material prices drop 8% in your region each March. Delay bulk orders."
- **Demand forecasting:** "Roofing demand spikes 3 weeks after storm season in your zip code. Pre-staff."
- **Supplier intelligence:** "2 of your peers switched away from Supplier X. Common reason: delays."

Fortune 500 companies pay millions for this intel. BITS delivers it privately for a monthly subscription.

### Why Nobody Can Copy This

This model only works because:
1. ✅ Hardware is already in the building (BITS box)
2. ✅ Data is already processed locally (privacy architecture)
3. ✅ Clients already trust BITS with their infrastructure

Cloud AI companies cannot replicate this. They'd need clients to upload sensitive financials and customer data to external servers. **No SMB owner does that.**

BITS has the relationship AND the hardware already inside the door. That's the moat.
