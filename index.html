Building Technology That Serves People

For nearly a decade, I've been immersed in the blockchain and AI space — studying the technology, building relationships, and understanding how it can truly serve real businesses and real people. As a Founding Director and Board Member of the Virginia Blockchain Council, I've worked at the intersection of policy, technology, and business — and I've seen firsthand what happens when small businesses get left behind.

The small businesses I work with every day — the local shops, the service providers, the family-run operations — are getting left behind. Big corporations had access to AI, automation, and enterprise tech that could transform their operations. The small guys? They were stuck with whatever Big Tech decided to serve them, often at the cost of their own data privacy.

Why Data Sovereignty Matters

I've seen what happens when you don't control your own foundation. When your business data lives on someone else's servers, you're at their mercy. When your business data lives on someone else's servers, processed by algorithms you can't see, you're building your future on borrowed ground.

That's why I founded BITS — not as another tech company trying to lock you into their ecosystem, but as a partner helping you build your own. Private AI that runs on your hardware. Blockchain solutions that you control. Technology infrastructure that's actually yours.

Humans and AI — Better Together

Here's what I believe: AI shouldn't replace people — it should amplify them. I've spent years watching skilled professionals do incredible work across every industry. Technology didn't make them obsolete; it gave them better tools. The experienced professional with AI in their corner works smarter and faster, but their expertise is still what makes the difference.

That's the vision behind everything we build at BITS. Not AI that takes your job, but AI that handles the tedious stuff so you can focus on what humans do best — creativity, relationships, judgment, and the kind of intuition that no algorithm can replicate.

Faith, Values, and the Long View

I'm a Bible-believing Christian, and that shapes how I approach everything — including business. There's a principle I hold close: build for the long term. In scripture, the wise man built his house on the rock. In business, that means building on principles that don't shift with every market trend — integrity, service, and genuine care for the people you work with.

Our Mission

To help small and mid-size businesses access enterprise-level technology while maintaining complete data sovereignty — because your data is yours, your privacy matters, and you shouldn't need a Fortune 500 budget to compete like one.
