Coaching Philosophy

Growth Starts Within

True transformation begins from the inside out. As a business and personal development coach, I believe that sustainable success isn't built on strategies alone—it's built on the foundation of who you become in the process.

My approach centers on the simple truth that personal development is the bedrock of professional achievement. When you develop emotional intelligence, clarify your goals, and cultivate inner resilience, the business strategies naturally fall into place. We don't chase success—we become the kind of person who attracts it.

Through one-on-one coaching sessions, we'll work together to uncover the mental frameworks and emotional patterns that shape your decisions. Whether you're navigating a career transition, building a business, or seeking deeper fulfillment, our journey will be rooted in authenticity, clarity, and lasting growth.

Build a strong inner foundation before building your empire. Self-awareness leads to better decisions, stronger relationships, and authentic leadership.

The Founder

The Story Behind BITS

George Mundin

Founder, BITS

From Construction Sites to Code

For over two decades, I built things the old-fashioned way—with brick, mortar, and calloused hands. As a Class A contractor in Virginia running GNW Contracting, I learned what it means to build something that lasts. Every foundation had to be solid. Every measurement precise. Because in construction, shortcuts don't just cost money—they put people at risk.

But somewhere along the way, I started noticing something. The small businesses I worked with—the restaurants, the shops, the local contractors—were getting left behind. Big corporations had access to AI, automation, and enterprise tech that could transform their operations. The little guys? They were stuck with whatever Big Tech decided to serve them, often at the cost of their own data privacy.

Why Data Sovereignty Matters

I've seen what happens when you don't control your own foundation. In construction, if you build on someone else's land, you're at their mercy. The same is true with technology. When your business data lives on someone else's servers, processed by algorithms you can't see, you're building your future on borrowed ground.

That's why I founded BITS—not as another tech company trying to lock you into their ecosystem, but as a partner helping you build your own. Private AI that runs on your hardware. Blockchain solutions that you control. Cloud infrastructure that's actually yours. Because your data is your business's foundation, and you should never have to ask permission to stand on it.

Humans and AI, Better Together

Here's what I believe: AI shouldn't replace people—it should amplify them. I've spent my career watching skilled craftsmen do incredible work. Technology didn't make them obsolete; it gave them better tools. The master mason with a laser level builds faster and more precisely than ever, but his expertise is still what makes the difference.

That's the vision behind everything we build at BITS. Not AI that takes your job, but AI that handles the tedious stuff so you can focus on what humans do best—creativity, relationships, judgment, and the kind of intuition that no algorithm can replicate. We're not building a future without people. We're building tools that let people do more of what makes them irreplaceable.

Faith, Values, and the Long View

I'm a Bible-believing Christian, and that shapes how I approach everything—including business. There's a principle I hold close: build for the long term. In scripture, the wise man built his house on the rock. In business, that means building on principles that don't shift with every market trend—integrity, service, and genuine care for the people you work with.

I've also found deep wisdom in the teachings of Neville Goddard, who emphasized the power of imagination and belief in shaping reality. It's not about positive thinking—it's about having a clear vision and holding to it, even when the path gets hard. That's how I approach BITS: with conviction that small businesses deserve better, and the determination to build solutions that actually serve them.

Our Mission

To help small and mid-size businesses access enterprise-level technology while maintaining complete data sovereignty—because your data is yours, your privacy matters, and you shouldn't need a Fortune 500 budget to compete like one.
