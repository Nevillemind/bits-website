FAQ

Common Questions

Everything else you need to know.

What exactly am I licensing when I buy access?
You're licensing access to anonymized data intelligence derived from verified operational records. No raw documents are ever shared — only aggregated patterns, trends, and insights. All content is privacy-preserved.
How are data suppliers paid?
Automatically, via smart contract. When a buyer licenses your dataset, the revenue splits instantly: 75-90% to you (depending on your plan), and the remainder to TORA Exchange as a platform fee. No invoicing, no delays.
Is my raw data ever exposed publicly?
Never. TORA Exchange is built on BITS DataVault architecture — document content never touches the blockchain. Only cryptographic hashes are anchored on-chain. Your records remain private, sovereign, and under your control.
What is a TORA asset exactly?
TORA (Tokenized Operational Record Asset) is a new asset class. It represents a corpus of business records that have been digitized, authenticated, hashed to the blockchain, and packaged into a licensable intelligence product.
Is this regulated? Do I need to worry about the SEC?
TORA Exchange operates as a data marketplace, not a securities exchange. Licensing data intelligence is commerce — the same model used by Bloomberg, S&P Global, and Dun & Bradstreet. For tokenized securities, we partner with Securitize's SEC-registered ATS.
Can I cancel anytime?
Yes. Monthly plans can be cancelled at any time with no penalty. Annual plans include a 30-day satisfaction guarantee. Enterprise agreements are governed by individual MSAs.
